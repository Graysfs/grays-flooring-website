Solid or Engineered:
What Is the Difference?

Solid hardwood is milled from a single piece of wood from top to bottom. Because of this, it can be sanded and refinished multiple times over its lifetime, making it a floor that can truly last generations. It is the most traditional option and carries the most natural character, variation, and depth of any wood flooring product.

Engineered hardwood features a real wood veneer over a multi-layer plywood or HDF core. This construction gives it greater dimensional stability, meaning it expands and contracts less with temperature and humidity changes. That makes engineered hardwood a better fit for areas where slight moisture fluctuations are a concern, including homes on concrete slab foundations, which is common throughout the East Valley.

Both are beautiful, both are real wood, and we install both. The right choice depends on your subfloor, your environment, and how long you plan to stay in the home. We walk you through it at the estimate.

How It Works
What Installation
Method Is Used?

We install hardwood using whichever method best suits your specific subfloor and situation. There is no one-size-fits-all approach here.

  • Nail / Staple Down — The traditional method for wood subfloors. Each plank is mechanically fastened for a solid, secure fit with no flex or movement.
  • Glue Down — Ideal for concrete subfloors or applications requiring a firm, permanent bond. Commonly used for engineered hardwood over slab.
  • Floating — A versatile method where planks click and lock together over the subfloor without being fastened directly to it. Allows for natural movement and is faster to install.

When installing over concrete, we assess each job individually and include either a vapor barrier, an underlayment, or both, depending on what the subfloor conditions require. This step is critical to protecting your hardwood investment from moisture migration and ensuring a stable, long-lasting result.
